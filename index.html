Girivalam Route Preview

Sacred Circumambulation of Arunachala Hill

Route Distance

The sacred Girivalam path covers approximately 14 kilometers around the base of the holy Arunachala Hill. This flat, well-paved path is suitable for devotees of all ages.

14 Kilometers Complete Circuit

Time Required

Typically takes 3 to 5 hours to complete, depending on your pace, rest breaks, and time spent in prayer at the various shrines along the route.

3-5 Hours Average Duration

Best Time

Full moon days (Pournami) are considered most auspicious. Many devotees prefer early morning or evening hours for a more peaceful experience.

Full Moon Days Most Auspicious

Temple History

The Arulmigu Arunachaleswarar Temple, also known as Annamalaiyar Temple, is magnificently situated at the foothills of the 2668 feet high Arunachala Hill in the sacred city of Tiruvannamalai. This ancient temple is a splendid example of South Indian (Dravidian) architecture and sculpture.

According to inscriptions found in the temple, it was built during the Early Chola period (9th Century AD) and was later expanded by Later Cholas, Hoysalas, and Vijayanagara Nayak rulers. The temple features the main sanctum, Ardha Mandapam, Maha Mandapam, and thousands of pillars showcasing exquisite carvings.

This is one of the Pancha Bhoota Stalams, representing the element of Fire (Agni). Lord Shiva is worshipped here as Arunachaleswarar in the form of Agni Lingam. The temple has four magnificent gopurams (gateway towers), with the eastern Rajagopuram standing at 217 feet tall with 13 tiers.
